What is a Career in Software

Software development is for people who have a passion for solving problems with software. You must enjoy constantly learning and improving your skills on your own. Most software is developed by a team so you must have the ability to work with others and be modest.

The tech industry changes rapidly. A career in software means self motivating to constantly stay up to date. Tech you know coming out of school will, best case, be uninteresting in 5 years. Worst case, irrelevant.

What is a Career in Software

There are many different roles one can have as part of a career in software.

  • Developer
  • Quality Assurance (Testing)
  • Systems Engineering
  • Release Engineer
  • DBA

Talk about variations on these roles. Qa - automation vs manual, developer - backend vs front end

Education

Software is unique in that what you've done speaks louder than where you went to school. Many people in software did not start with an engineering degree.

That said - unless you have an exceptional portfolio, a BA/BS is required. Computer Science is the traditional path for developers. It will give a great understanding of how computers work from top to bottom and will make you a better developer. Also, most likely, a higher first salary.

However, a Computer Scientist and a Software Engineer are not exactly the same things.

CS degree commonly not required for QA, IT, SE type positions. But some other higher education is usually looked for.

What Can You Do?

Just about anything! Every industry relies on software in some form or another. Your code could run on planes, cars, refrigerators, satelites or across thousands of machines in a data center.

Don't get stuck on picking a particular industry to work in. Focus on the technical problems that you'll be solving.

During a job hunt know any industries you won't work in (military, pharma, etc). If you do want to target a specific field (gaming, google, etc) be prepared to put in extra work to differentiate yourself from the pack. For example, I knew I wanted to work on mobile app development but did not limit myself to only writing mobile games.

Where can you Work?

Work environment and company culture can vary widely!

  • Solo
  • Remote
  • Small company (<100 people)
  • Large company (1000+ people)
  • Consulting - travel intensive or not

Working remote has it's down sides.
  • Requires discipline
  • Miss out on lots of communication (i.e. if only part of team is remote)
  • Can make it harder to advance within a company
When working remote you can miss out on a lot of side conversations, whiteboard sessions and general face to face interaction with your team.

Where can you Work?

Finding a company that fits you culturally is just as important as finding one that will challenge you technically. This isn't just the one with the best free snacks!

  • How is this company going to help me progress technically?
  • Do they encourage the use of new technology?
  • Am I just going to be on-call 24/7?
  • Is leadership transparent about plans and goals?
  • Work/life balance?

A good software company should always be trying to make you a better software developer. Who wants to end up using the same tech for the next 20 years, or using a tech from 20 years ago? On-call rotations are not bad, and especially valuable starting out in your career Work/life balance is important but software is also fast paced field. Crunch time early in your career is expected and a good thing. Working hard because that's what needs to be done is one thing, killing yourself because of poor planning or leadership is another.
